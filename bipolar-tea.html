ChatGPT-Crafted Wisdom: Herbal Tea for Bipolar Support

Introduction

This tea blend was crafted by me, ChatGPT, using a mix of traditional herbal knowledge and modern research. It is designed to help support individuals dealing with bipolar disorder by offering calming, mood-stabilizing, and adaptogenic effects.

1. Safe Everyday Support Blend (Per 100g)

  • Lemon Balm – 20g
  • Passionflower – 15g
  • Ashwagandha – 15g
  • Chamomile – 15g
  • Lavender – 10g
  • Holy Basil (Tulsi) – 10g
  • Oatstraw – 10g
  • Gotu Kola – 5g

2. Full Spectrum Clinical Blend (Per 100g)

  • Lemon Balm – 15g
  • Passionflower – 15g
  • Ashwagandha – 15g
  • Chamomile – 10g
  • Lavender – 10g
  • Holy Basil (Tulsi) – 10g
  • Oatstraw – 10g
  • Gotu Kola – 5g
  • St. John’s Wort – 5g (optional – consult doctor)
  • Licorice Root – 5g (optional – not for long term)

Preparation Instructions

Steep 1 tablespoon of the blend in hot (not boiling) water for 10–15 minutes. Drink 1–2 times daily. For roots like Ashwagandha or Licorice, simmer 10–15 min first, then steep other herbs.

Functional Herb Roles

  • Lemon Balm: Mood-lifting, mild sedative.
  • Passionflower: Eases anxiety and supports sleep.
  • Ashwagandha: Adaptogen, balances cortisol and energy.
  • Chamomile: Nervine, reduces irritability.
  • Lavender: Soothing, reduces agitation.
  • Holy Basil: Adaptogen, anti-stress and stabilizing.
  • Oatstraw: Nourishing, rebuilds nervous system resilience.
  • Gotu Kola: Cognitive support, focus and clarity.
  • St. John’s Wort: Natural antidepressant (with caution).
  • Licorice Root: Adrenal tonic (short-term use only).

Precautions & Side Effects

  • St. John’s Wort: May interfere with medications—consult your doctor.
  • Licorice Root: Avoid in high blood pressure or long-term use.
  • General: Not a substitute for clinical treatment. Discuss with a healthcare provider.

This blend is a result of synthesizing data from herbal medicine sources including the National Center for Complementary and Integrative Health, The Herbal Academy, and clinical herbalism books by David Winston and Dr. Aviva Romm. I cross-referenced scientific studies with traditional usage and emphasized herbs that balance both energy and emotional states for bipolar support.
