Tea Formula (Per Liter)

  • 1 tsp Green Tea
  • 1 tsp Turmeric Root (or ½ tsp powder)
  • 1 tsp Ginger Root
  • 1 tsp Dandelion Root
  • 1 tsp Holy Basil (Tulsi)
  • 1 tsp Red Clover
  • 1 tsp Milk Thistle Seed (crushed)
  • Optional: 1 tsp Reishi Mushroom (dried, chopped)
  • + Pinch of black pepper (to enhance turmeric absorption)

Instructions: Simmer roots (ginger, turmeric, dandelion, reishi, milk thistle) for 15–20 minutes. Add green tea, red clover, tulsi — steep for 10 more minutes. Strain and enjoy warm.

Recommended: 1–2 cups daily, fresh-brewed. Avoid during pregnancy or while on medications without professional guidance.

Possible Side Effects

  • Green Tea: Contains caffeine; may cause jitteriness, insomnia.
  • Turmeric: May cause stomach irritation in high doses; avoid with gallstones.
  • Ginger: May thin blood; use caution with anticoagulants.
  • Milk Thistle: Can cause bloating or digestive upset.
  • Dandelion Root: Acts as a diuretic; not suitable for those on diuretics or with kidney issues.
  • Red Clover: Contains phytoestrogens; avoid with hormone-sensitive conditions.
  • Holy Basil: Generally safe; avoid during pregnancy without consultation.
  • Reishi Mushroom: In high amounts, may cause dry mouth, dizziness, or upset stomach.

Precautions

This tea is for general wellness and prevention, not a substitute for medical treatment. Consult a healthcare provider before use if you are:

  • Undergoing cancer treatment (chemotherapy, radiation, hormone therapy)
  • Taking blood thinners or medications affecting the liver or kidneys
  • Pregnant or breastfeeding
